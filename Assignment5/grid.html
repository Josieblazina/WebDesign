I Meant to Tell You

A participatory postcard project that invites people to write the letters they could never send — and then gives them the opportunity to send them, or not.

Type Participatory Art
Medium Print, Installation
Year 2025
By Josie Blazina

The Concept

Most people have a letter they wrote with no intention to send. Maybe yours is tucked away in a drawer, maybe it only ever existed in your head, repeating hundreds of times in the shower, on a long car ride, or after being reminded of something you convinced yourself you got over.

It's addressed to someone you have loved, or someone you've lost, or someone you have grown away from. It says the one thing you never could quite get yourself to say out loud. You didn't send it. Perhaps you can't. Perhaps you never will. But it deserved to exist.

The Project

Visual materials and design work developed for the installation.

How It Works

At accessible public locations like coffee shops, university campuses, and parks, people find a space set up with various vintage postcards and a dropbox. The postcards are ruled for writing and addressed to "whoever it may reach." Next to the dropbox are two inked stamps: SEND and HOLD.

The participant stamps their chosen postcard and drops it in the box. If they stamp SEND, it gets mailed. If they stamp HOLD, the postcard joins an ongoing installation at the location — pinned to the wall, suspended from the ceiling, visible to anyone who passes by. A mural of unsaid things.

The end deliverable has multiple possible outcomes: a hot spot location-based art installation of the collected letters, and a purchasable book collecting some of the most remarkable cards, preserved exactly as they were written.

The Installation

Postcards held, displayed, and returned to the world.
